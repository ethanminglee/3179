Melbourne: A Look at Transport

A deep dive into how Melbournians transport to, from and around the CBD

Background

Melbourne (Naarm) is the capital of the Australia state of Victoria, and is the second most populous city in Australia. The central business district, CBD, of Melbourne has an area of 37.7 square kilometers, and a population of 149,615. Melbourne is a hub for intercity, intracity and regional travel, aided by its extensive public transport system and freeway network. Road-based transport constitutes the majority of travel through the CBD, but other dominant modes of transport include walking, cycling and public transport. Home to the world's largest tram network, the public transport system also includes trains and buses.
